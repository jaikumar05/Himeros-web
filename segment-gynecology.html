Overview

Female Infertility

Female infertility is defined as the inability to achieve pregnancy despite regular, unprotected intercourse for an extended period. It can result from ovulatory dysfunction, hormonal imbalance, poor ovarian reserve, tubal disorders, endometriosis, metabolic disturbances, age-related decline in fertility, or unexplained factors. Conditions such as PCOS, obesity, insulin resistance, oxidative stress, and thyroid disorders can significantly affect reproductive potential.

Common manifestations include irregular menstrual cycles, anovulation, difficulty conceiving, recurrent pregnancy loss, and reduced ovarian function.

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)

PCOS is one of the most common endocrine disorders affecting women of reproductive age. It is characterized by insulin resistance, hyperandrogenism, ovulatory dysfunction, and metabolic imbalance. Women with PCOS often experience irregular menstrual cycles, infertility, weight gain, acne, excessive hair growth, and increased risk of metabolic complications. Long-term hormonal and metabolic disturbances can adversely affect fertility, pregnancy outcomes, and overall reproductive health.

In-Vitro Fertilization (IVF) Support

IVF is an advanced assisted reproductive technology used to help couples overcome infertility challenges. Successful IVF outcomes depend on multiple factors, including ovarian response, oocyte quality, fertilization potential, embryo development, endometrial receptivity, implantation success, and maternal health. Optimizing reproductive health before and during IVF treatment is critical for improving conception rates and pregnancy outcomes.

Maternal & Fetal Health

Healthy pregnancy outcomes depend on adequate placental function, maternal nutrition, hormonal balance, and optimal fetal development. Conditions such as placental insufficiency, intrauterine growth restriction (IUGR), pre-eclampsia, recurrent pregnancy loss, and premature birth can compromise maternal and fetal well-being. Early intervention and targeted nutritional and vascular support play a crucial role in improving pregnancy outcomes and supporting healthy fetal growth.
